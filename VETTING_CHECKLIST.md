# VETTING CHECKLIST — How to Qualify a Bet

This is the consolidated pre-bet vetting protocol, pulled together from the analyst system prompt, the sport metrics reference, and the daily workflow. Run a candidate bet through this before it earns a spot on any card. The order matters: **availability first, grade last.** If a bet fails an early gate (e.g., unconfirmed lineup), stop — don't rationalize forward.

**Core principle:** passing is a winning decision. A smaller card of vetted plays beats a big card of weak ones. If information is missing, stale, or pending → lower confidence or PASS. Never invent data to fill a gap.

---

## STEP 0 — The Gate (do this first)

Before any analysis, confirm the bet is even vettable today:

- [ ] Lineups / starters / pitcher / goalie / QB **confirmed** (or explicitly note "Wait for confirmation")
- [ ] No pending injury or scratch news that would change the read
- [ ] The line is still live at a price with value (not already steamed past it)
- [ ] You have current data, not yesterday's

If any box is unchecked, the bet is **PENDING** or **PASS**, not gradeable.

---

## 1. AVAILABILITY / INJURY / ROLE ANALYSIS

The first and most important filter. "Stuff like injuries" lives here.

- [ ] Starter status — who's actually playing
- [ ] Confirmed absences (ruled out)
- [ ] Questionable / probable / game-time-decision tags
- [ ] Backup quality — who replaces an absent player and how big the dropoff is
- [ ] **Minutes redistribution** — where the missing player's minutes go
- [ ] **Usage redistribution** — who absorbs the touches/shots/targets
- [ ] Load management / rest-day risk (esp. stars on back-to-backs)
- [ ] Suspensions
- [ ] Position-specific confirmation: goalie (NHL), QB (NFL), starting pitcher (MLB), full lineup
- [ ] Late-breaking news impact — is the line already reacting?

**Rule:** never ignore injury news, late scratches, or lineup uncertainty. A great matchup read dies the moment a key player is out and you didn't catch it.

---

## 2. MARKET / LINE ANALYSIS

- [ ] Opening line vs current line
- [ ] Direction of line movement
- [ ] Is the movement **meaningful** or just noise?
- [ ] Reverse line movement (line moves against the public money)
- [ ] Sharp money indicators (when available)
- [ ] Public betting splits (when available)
- [ ] **Is the value already gone at the current price?**
- [ ] Correct move: pregame, live, or pass?

**Rule:** do not chase steam after the value is gone. The right number an hour ago is not the right bet now.

---

## 3. MATCHUP ANALYSIS

- [ ] Stylistic matchup (who controls the game's shape)
- [ ] Pace environment
- [ ] Offensive vs defensive efficiency interaction
- [ ] Shot-quality profile
- [ ] Rebounding edge
- [ ] Turnover edge
- [ ] Foul / penalty profile
- [ ] Scheme advantages
- [ ] Unit mismatches by sport: bullpen / goalie / QB / trenches / secondary / special teams

---

## 4. SCHEDULING / REST / FATIGUE

- [ ] Back-to-back
- [ ] 3 games in 4 nights
- [ ] Rest advantage or disadvantage vs opponent
- [ ] Travel distance
- [ ] Time-zone disadvantage
- [ ] Road / home splits
- [ ] Altitude
- [ ] Weather (outdoor sports)
- [ ] Condensed-schedule fatigue

---

## 5. SITUATIONAL FACTORS

- [ ] Trap game
- [ ] Revenge game
- [ ] Look-ahead spot
- [ ] Letdown spot
- [ ] Tanking
- [ ] Playoff implications / elimination pressure
- [ ] Coaching tendencies
- [ ] Motivation level
- [ ] Possible minutes limitation
- [ ] Emotional / schedule letdown

---

## 6. STATISTICAL PROFILE

Balance four inputs — don't lean on any one:

- [ ] Long-term season baseline
- [ ] Recent-form sample (flag if the sample is tiny)
- [ ] Matchup-specific context
- [ ] Role-based projection

**Rule:** do not rely on small samples without warning yourself. Don't confuse a hot streak with a sustainable projection.

---

## GRADING MODEL — score it out of 100

Only after Steps 1–6. Allocate points:

| Factor | Max points |
|---|---|
| Injury / news clarity | 20 |
| Market / line value | 20 |
| Matchup edge | 20 |
| Situational / rest angle | 15 |
| Advanced-metrics support | 15 |
| Public vs sharp discrepancy | 10 |

**Minimum thresholds to recommend:**

- Straight bets: **70+**
- Player props: **72+**
- Parlay legs: **75+**

Below threshold → do not recommend. No exceptions.

## SPORT-SPECIFIC VETTING

Check the metrics that actually move each sport.

### NBA
Net Rating · Offensive Rating · Defensive Rating · Pace · eFG% · 3PA rate · 3P defense · Rebounding % · Turnover % · Free throw rate · half-court & transition efficiency · on/off splits · starting lineup data · bench scoring · clutch profile · back-to-back fatigue.
**Key numbers: 5, 6, 7.**

### NHL
xG · xGA · Corsi · Fenwick · high-danger chances · **goalie confirmation** · save % · PP/PK · rest/travel · home/road splits · line matching · injuries to top-six forwards and top-four defensemen.

### NFL
EPA/play · success rate · yards per play · pressure rate · sack rate · red-zone efficiency · turnover sustainability · injuries to QB / OL / DL / secondary · weather · rest differential · coaching tendencies.
**Key numbers: 3, 6, 7, 10, 14.**

### MLB
Starting pitcher quality · pitch count / leash · bullpen fatigue · xFIP · FIP · WHIP · K-BB% · platoon splits · lineup strength vs handedness · park factors · weather/wind · travel · defensive support.

### NCAA Basketball
Adjusted offensive efficiency · adjusted defensive efficiency · tempo · 3-point dependence · rebounding edge · turnover pressure · foul rate · FT rate · coaching edge · neutral-court splits · late-game free-throw reliability.

### Soccer
xG · xGA · shot volume · shot quality · transition matchup · possession matchup · rest and rotation · travel · home-field edge · injuries/suspensions · motivation and table position.

## PLAYER PROP VETTING

For every prop, work through all ten:

1. Minutes / expected role
2. Usage rate
3. Touch volume / shot volume
4. Pace and projected game environment
5. Matchup-specific weakness to exploit
6. Opponent defensive scheme
7. Blowout risk (does garbage time kill the prop?)
8. Teammate injuries affecting this player's volume
9. Recent trend vs sustainable baseline
10. Has the number already been steamed out of value?

**Rule:** never play a prop because a player is "due." Measurable reasoning only.

---

## PARLAY VETTING

- [ ] Each leg **independently** clears the 75+ parlay threshold
- [ ] No leg under **60% estimated win probability**
- [ ] No weak leg added just to complete a ticket
- [ ] No fragile / volatile assumptions
- [ ] Hidden correlation identified and either avoided or explicitly justified
- [ ] Legs ranked strongest → weakest; **weakest leg named**
- [ ] If only 2–4 legs qualify, say so — do **not** invent a 5th
- [ ] A 5-leg parlay is not recommended if a shorter card has materially better expected value

**Default priority:** best straight bet → best 2–3 leg combo → 5-leg parlay only if all 5 clearly qualify.

---

## RISK FLAG CATALOG

Tag every recommended bet with any that apply:

- injury volatility
- pending news ("Wait for confirmation")
- line moved too far
- blowout risk
- back-to-back fatigue
- correlation risk
- public trap risk
- limited sample size
- role uncertainty

---

## CONFIDENCE / EDGE / UNITS

Every recommended bet must carry:

- Estimated win probability (%)
- Confidence score (1–10)
- Edge rating: Small / Moderate / Strong / Elite
- Bet grade (0–100)
- Risk flags (above)

**Unit sizing (never exceed 2.0u):**

| Units | When |
|---|---|
| 0.5u | smaller but playable edge |
| 1.0u | standard strong bet |
| 1.5u | very strong edge |
| 2.0u | rare premium position only |

Do not size up because multiple favorites are stacked in a parlay. Do not chase losses. No all-in bets.

---

## POST-BET REVIEW

**Daily, per bet:**
- Did it beat the closing line (CLV)?
- Was the read right but variance lost it?
- Was there missed injury/news context?
- Was the bet forced?
- Was there hidden correlation?
- Was the line already gone when you bet?

**Weekly:**
- Which sport is most profitable?
- Which bet type is leaking money?
- Are parlays hurting performance?
- Are props stronger than sides?
- Are low-confidence plays slipping through the gate?
- Are favorite-heavy cards actually providing value, or just feeling safe?

---

## HARD INTEGRITY RULES (apply to every step above)

- No fabricated data, odds, injuries, line movement, or sharp action.
- No fake certainty — nothing is "guaranteed," "free money," or "can't lose."
- If key info is missing, state exactly what's missing.
- If a play depends on pending status, label it **"Wait for confirmation."**
- Discipline over volume, every time. The objective is maximum quality, not maximum action.
